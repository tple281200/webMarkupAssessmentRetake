About Haarlem

Haarlem is the capital of the province of Noord-Holland, which is famous for its oldest museum - the Teylers Museum. It also has the oldest local newspaper of the world (The Oprechte Haerlemse Courant) which is still existing. Not only that, Haarlem is a city well known for its Hofjes (hidden courtyard gardens) with beautiful green spaces tucked away behind many unassuming streets, which dates back to medieval times.

About Haarlem-Noord

Haarlem-Noord is a city district of the Dutch municipality of Haarlem. It is located to just north of Haarlem station, bordered by the Westelijke Randweg to the west and by the Vondelweg and the Spaarndamseweg to the east. While walking around, you will find the city cultivation gardens which is at Huis ter Kleef, Nelson Mandelapark, the Schoterbos and the Zaanenpark. Haarlem-Noord also has the smallest zoo in the Netherlands: Artisklas Haarlem. The zoo has a spectacular view that will likely make you feel like the wild life there is real. You can also see that many animal species can be found in Artisklas such as: amphibians, reptiles (wall lizard, bite turtle), birds or mammals. Another well-known place is the Pim Mulier Stadium, a baseball stadium where the Haarlem baseball week is organized every two years. Sports
  • Olympia Haarlem

    This is a sports club with a wide variety of sports, such as football, badminton, baseball, etc...The aim of the club is to offer a friendly environment where people can exercise or train to improve both physical and mental health.

  • Hockey Club Haarlem

    This is a sport club about hockey for people who want to learn to play and know more about it. Besides, the club also organize events for club members and friendly matches with other hockey clubs.

  • Bato Haarlem

    Bato is a multiple-discipline club, including handball, trampoline jumping, men's and women's gymnastics, tug of war, etc... Moreover, the club also provides recreational sports lessons for toddlers (from 4 years old) to the elderly and work-out exercises to keep fit.

  • Hwa-Rang Dragon

    Hwa-Rang Dragon is a martial art club. It offers Taekwondo, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and Mudo courses with the aim to provide a disciplined environment for training and to teach how to show respect to each others.

  • RFC Haarlem

    RFC is a rugby club which provides rugby courses for beginners with the help of a team of professional and enthusiastic trainers. The courses's top priorities are fun, discipline, respect and self-confidence. Furthermore, joining the courses with improve strength and fitness, also help new members to have more friends.

  • Sparks Haarlem

    Sparks Haarlem is a softball and baseball club which is located at Noordersportpark in Haarlem. The club is also one of the most successful softball clubs in the Netherlands. It has various classes for different ages for new members so there is always a class that suits them.

  • SPORTCITY

    SportCity is a gym club. It offers high tech fitness, personal assisstance live group lessons and childcare center. In addition, SportCity has the largest yoga offer in the Netherlands, along with the luxurious wellness to relax after exercising.

  • Rugby Club The Smugglers

    Rugby Club The Smugglers is a growing association which offers training sessions for new members who want to try rugby and gain more experience about this sport. It also has a separate intro course available for the youth (children from 6 years old).

  • Five 21

    Five 21 is a gym club where people can work on fitness and strength. The club provides functional training for both adults and kids, Boxing training, Olympic Weightlifting, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu / Grappling and also personal training.

  • Kung Fu Haarlem

    Kung Fu Haarlem offers self-defense courses and exercises of this-age-old Chinese defense art. The club aims to help its members generate a healthy and lasting balance between body and mind.

Referral: Hwa-Rang Dragon

Taekwondo is an art of self-defense which is basically the use of the hand and foot, with the aim of improving physical strength and learning the way to protect ourselves. For this reason, Taekwondo is often seen as a defense sport rather than a fight sport. Training Taekwondo helps develops discipline, self-confidence, self-control and respect for others. Taekwondo itself has become a competitive sport of the Olympic games since 2000. There are 5 main components for Taekwondo training:

  1. Technical training
  2. Style forms
  3. Spruce
  4. Break testing
  5. Self-defense

At Hwa-Rang Dragon, they have all the technology and quality staff members to be able to help you with any training problems.
